From Nanomaterials to Intelligent Devices: Printed Electronics, Biosensors, and Beyond

We engineer next-generation electronics and sensors by merging materials science, biochemistry, scalable printing, and AI. Our work spans flexible devices, neuromorphic and memory elements, tactile arrays, and sustainable electronics for Society 5.0.

Our Research

Printed Nanomaterials for Flexible Electronics

MXene, CNT, and graphene inks for scalable screen-printing of high-performance films, enabling wearable devices, flexible circuits, and large-area systems.

Neuromorphic & Memory Devices

Printed resistive switching and synaptic devices for brain-inspired, energy-efficient computation in robotics and intelligent sensing.

Intelligent Tactile & Health Sensors

Flexible tactile arrays and wearable monitors coupled with machine learning for real-time perception in healthcare, rehabilitation, and robotics.

Sustainable Materials & Processes

Eco-friendly inks, recyclable substrates, and low-energy fabrication to make advanced electronics scalable, affordable, and sustainable.

Past Projects (Biosensors & Food Safety) Click to expand

Antibacterial Drug Sensors

Electrochemical monitoring of antibacterial drugs to inform stewardship and resistance studies.

Anti-epileptic Drug Sensors

Therapeutic drug monitoring for optimized dosing and better clinical outcomes.

Food Toxin Sensors

Rapid detection of histamine/inosine to prevent foodborne illness and protect public health.
