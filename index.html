About the Lab

Sensing platforms, from material to device

AESD Lab develops flexible and printed electronic devices, electrochemical sensors, and nanomaterials for electronics — building device structures and material systems for measurement and monitoring applications across health, food, and environmental sensing.

The lab is based in the College of Systems Engineering and Science at Shibaura Institute of Technology and is closely connected with the Innovative Global Program, where research activity feeds directly into English-medium engineering education.

Focus Areas

Research snapshot

Four connected threads — from the materials and printing processes that make a sensor, to the chemistry that makes it selective, to the device physics that makes it efficient.

Flexible & printed devices

Device structures and fabrication processes — including low-cost, screen-printed resists and electrodes — for flexible, application-oriented electronics on plastic, paper, and ceramic substrates.

Electrochemical & optical sensors

Molecularly-imprinted and paste-electrode sensing platforms for therapeutic drug monitoring, food freshness, and biospeckle optical sensing for agricultural and environmental samples.

Nanomaterials for electronics

Oxide-, carbon-, and 2D-material systems — including ZnO, MoO3, and black phosphorus — engineered for gas sensing, optoelectronics, and thin-film photovoltaics.

Intelligent & wearable sensing

Self-powered and wearable sensor concepts, and the integration of sensor data with machine learning for real-time food-safety and environmental monitoring.
