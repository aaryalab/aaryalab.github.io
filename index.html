Engineering Novel Sensors for a Healthier and Safer World

Welcome to the Advanced Electronics and Sensor Devices Lab. We operate at the intersection of physics, chemistry, and electronics to create cutting-edge biosensors and nanomaterial-based devices. Our mission is to develop sensitive, reliable technology for critical applications in healthcare, drug monitoring, and food safety.

Join Our Team

Our Research

Antibacterial Drug Sensors

To combat the rise of bacterial resistance, we are developing electrochemical sensors with modified carbon to rapidly and accurately detect antibacterial drugs, providing crucial data for monitoring drug usage in healthcare.

Anti-epileptic Drug Sensors

We aim to improve epilepsy management by creating sensors that precisely measure anti-epileptic drug levels in a patient's system, enabling optimized dosing and better therapeutic outcomes.

Food Toxin Sensors

To ensure the safety of our food supply, this project focuses on electrochemical sensors that can quickly identify toxins like histamine and inosine, preventing foodborne illness and protecting public health.

Meet the Principal Investigator

Aaryashree (Aarya)

Associate Professor

Dr. Aaryashree is an educator and researcher with over seven years of experience in nanomaterial-based electronics and sensor design. Her work is driven by a philosophy that "Education encompasses not only formal university studies, but also the acquisition of new knowledge, the unlearning of outdated concepts, and the experiences gained from the process of learning."

When not in the lab, she enjoys discussing geopolitics and current events with her partner over Indian tea or working on DIY projects.
