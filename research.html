Research

Sensing platforms, from material to device

AESD Lab studies flexible and printed electronic devices, electrochemical and optical sensors, nanomaterials for electronics, and the integration of sensor data with intelligent analysis — spanning device fabrication, materials chemistry, and applied measurement.

Flexible & printed electronic devices

Device structures based on printable materials and flexible substrates — plastic, paper, and ceramic — with emphasis on low-cost fabrication, durability, and integration into practical sensing platforms.

Recent work: “A Robust, Ultra-Low-Cost Workflow for Fabricating Flexible Electrochemical Devices Using a Screen-Printed Enamel Resist,” LOPEC 2026, Munich.

Electrochemical & optical sensors

Sensing platforms for chemical and biological measurement, including molecularly-imprinted carbon-paste electrodes for therapeutic drug monitoring and food freshness, and biospeckle optical sensing for agricultural samples.

Recent work: “A Disposable Sensor Chip Using a Paste Electrode with Surface-Imprinted Graphite Particles for Rapid and Reagentless Monitoring of Theophylline,” Molecules (2022).

Nanomaterials for electronics & sensing

Oxide-, carbon-, and 2D-material systems — including ZnO, MoO₃, and black phosphorus — for gas sensing, optoelectronics, and thin-film photovoltaic applications.

Recent work: “Recent Advances in 2D Black Phosphorus-Based Materials for Gas Sensing Applications,” Journal of Materials Chemistry C (2021).

Intelligent & wearable sensing

Self-powered and wearable sensor concepts, and the integration of sensor outputs with machine learning for real-time food-safety and environmental monitoring.

Recent work: “Integrating Machine Learning with Electrochemical Sensors for Intelligent Food Safety Monitoring,” Artificial Intelligence Chemistry (2026).
